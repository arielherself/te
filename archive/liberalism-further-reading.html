    Liberalism: further reading

    This essay benefited from the insights and assistance of a great many people. In particular, Alexander Zevin of City University of New York, author of a forthcoming independent history of this newspaper ("The World According to The Economist", to be published by Verso Books), was an outstanding guide to our history.

    The essay has drawn on the following books and articles:

    Books
    “Reordering the World: Essays on Liberalism and Empire”, by Duncan Bell. Princeton University Press, 2016

    “Refuge: Transforming a Broken Refugee System”, by Alexander Betts and Paul Collier. Penguin Books, 2017

    “Exodus: How Migration is Changing Our World”, by Paul Collier. Oxford University Press, 2013

    “The Future of Capitalism: Facing the New Anxieties”, by Paul Collier. Allen Lane, 2018

    “Why Liberalism Failed”, by Patrick J. Deneen. Yale University Press, 2018

    “The Pursuit of Reason: The Economist 1843-1993”, by Ruth Dudley Edwards. Hamish Hamilton, 1993

    “Liberalism: The Life of an Idea”, by Edmund Fawcett, Princeton University Press, 2014

    “The New Urban Crisis”, by Richard Florida. OneWorld, 2017

    “Success and Luck: Good Fortune and the Myth of Meritocracy”, by Robert H. Frank. Princeton University Press, 2016

    “Liberalism: A Very Short Introduction”, by Michael Freeden. Oxford University Press, 2015

    “Capitalism and Freedom”, by Milton Friedman. University of Chicago Press, 1962

    “The Road to Somewhere”, by David Goodhart. C. Hurst & Co, 2017

    “The Limits of the Market”, by Paul de Grauwe, Oxford University Press, 2017

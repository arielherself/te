    Best of our “Babbage” podcast in 2022

    Ten highlights from our science-and-technology podcast

    “BABBAGE”, The Economist’s science-and-technology podcast, does a deep dive on a different topic every week. Our producer picks ten essential listens from 2022.

    In January, we investigated what could be behind new cases of Havana syndrome, an illness mainly plaguing Western diplomats. The podcast looked at the main suspects, from microwave weapons to psychological explanations.

    A theme we explored from several different angles in 2022 was how wearable technology, such as smartwatches, could transform health care. The first episode was released in January.

    2022 was a rocky year for crypto. In February, we explained what web3 is, why backers say it will reinvent cyberspace, and why scepticism has been growing.

    Throughout the year, we examined the role of technology in the war in Ukraine, including how tactical nuclear weapons could be used by Russia.

    2022 was the year the covid-19 pandemic began to fade away in much of the world. Our attention turned to how to fight other infectious diseases, such as malaria. Could vaccines or genetically modified mosquitoes vanquish one of the world’s most deadly diseases?

    In April, the Large Hadron Collider (pictured) at CERN became operational again after a three-year break for maintenance and upgrades. For a series of special episodes, we travelled to the Franco-Swiss border to explain what the particle collider could reveal about the fundamental building blocks of the universe.

    This year the energy crisis has made it tougher to act on climate change. In April, we asked if the nuclear-energy industry would be revived.

    A growing number of clinics now offer ketamine—also used illegally as a party drug—as a treatment for mental-health conditions. We explored the therapy’s potential.

    In June, a Google engineer claimed that an AI chatbot was sentient. We weighed in on the debate with some of the world’s leading experts on AI and consciousness.

    NASA launched the world’s most powerful rocket, the Space Launch System, in 2022. As the private-sector “New Space” industry blossoms, we asked: why did NASA bother?
